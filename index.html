Welcome to GEE-ESS'24

We are happy to invite you to attend the GEE-ESS'24, the premier European Spring School dedicated to exploring and mastering the Google Earth Engine. Hosted at the BOKU Türkenschanze campus in Vienna, this program aims to provide an enriching experience from the 2nd to the 5th of April, strategically positioned the week after Easter and two weeks before EGU, seeking to optimize travel for attendees.

This initiative is a unique collaborative effort led by the ÖAW (Austrian Academy of Sciences) and BOKU (University of Natural Resources and Life Sciences, Vienna), in partnership with the University of Valencia. Led by Google Earth Engine Developer Experts, we aim to provide participants with:

  • Comprehensive insights into the functionalities and capabilities of the Google Earth Engine.
  • Hands-on sessions, workshops, and collaborative projects to harness the power of geospatial data.
  • Networking with fellow enthusiasts, researchers, and professionals, and learn from Google Developer and leading experts.

Program Structure

Days 1 & 2: Lectures and hands-on sessions, providing participants with foundational knowledge and practical skills in Google Earth Engine.

Days 3 & 4 (Morning): Group projects, where participants will collaborate to apply their knowledge and tackle real-world challenges using Google Earth Engine.

Day 4 (Afternoon): Group presentations focusing on the approach, technical challenges, and solutions rather than the content of the projects.

Accommodation & Travel

Please note that we have not arranged any specific accommodations for the event, and participants are responsible for their own lodging arrangements. Vienna offers a wide range of hotels to suit all budgets.

For those participating in EGU, it may be more cost-effective to stay in Vienna for the extra week. We have prioritized offering this spring school at a reduced rate rather than incurring the additional expense of renting a more exclusive venue.

Traveling to Vienna is convenient, as the city is well connected by both train and airplane. Major train stations and the international airport provide easy access to the city from various locations.

We appreciate your understanding and look forward to welcoming you to Vienna!

Speakers

Emma Izquierdo-Verdiguier

BOKU (University of Natural Resources and Life Sciences, Vienna)


My research interests are the use of Earth Observation data and cloud computing environment for land surface phenology. My previous research focused on machine learning applied to remote sensing data. In particular, nonlinear feature extraction based on kernel methods and on automatic object identification and classification using multispectral images.

Mathieu Gravey

Junior Group Leader at IGF-ÖAW (Institute For Interdisciplinary Mountain Research - Austrian Academy of Sciences)


I specialized in geostatistics, remote sensing, and machine learning. My current research includes developing novel passive sensing technology and collaborating on various subjects such as vegetation evolution, growing season analysis, and bird tracking. I am is dedicated to cutting-edge solutions and community engagement through open and accessible software.

Álvaro Moreno Martínez

Senior Post Doctoral researcher at the University of Valencia


My research has been mainly focused on remote sensing applications in vegetation. I have been actively involved in the development of physical and statistical models and the implementation of operational methodologies for the study of vegetation cover through satellite imagery at different spatial/temporal scales.
